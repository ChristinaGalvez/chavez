About Cesar Chavez

Cesar Chavez (1927-1993) was a civil rights activist and labor leader who co-founded the United Farm Workers (UFW) in 1962. He dedicated his life to fighting for the rights of farm workers who were often mistreated and underpaid. He organized protests, strikes, and boycotts to bring attention to the injustices faced by farm workers and to demand better working conditions and wages.

Accomplishments

Co-founded the United Farm Workers (UFW) in 1962

Organized the Delano grape strike, which lasted for five years and resulted in the first collective bargaining agreement for farm workers

Advocated for the rights of farm workers and helped improve their working conditions and wages

Used non-violent tactics such as fasting to protest against unjust treatment of farm workers

Quotes

"The fight is never about grapes or lettuce. It is always about people."

"Preservation of one's own culture does not require contempt or disrespect for other cultures."

"Once social change begins, it cannot be reversed. You cannot un-educate the person who has learned to read. You cannot humiliate the person who feels pride. You cannot oppress the people who are not afraid anymore."

The Artist Andrea Torres

Andrea Torres is a Central Valley queer Chicanx artist and muralist, born in Fresno, California.

She grew up alternately between Los Reyes, Michoacan and Fresno, until the age of 8, when she moved permanently to Fresno.

She graduated from California State University, Fresno with double bachelor degrees in Art and French. She is currently pursuing a master’s degree in Studio Art at Fresno State University while simultaneously taking on the role of muralists and educator with Fresno Unified.

She has been a sought after artist by local art agencies and civic groups requesting her participation in community beautification projects. These experiences have only encouraged her love and support for public art. Some of the many local agencies she has worked with include: the Urbanists Collective, Every Neighborhood Partnership, Dulce UpFront, the Fresno Arts Council, and various others.

For the past seven years, Andrea has dedicated her time to developing murals at local schools and organizations, such as Barrios Unidos and Yo’Ville Community Garden.

She enjoys working within multi-generational settings and flourishes in environments where creativity is allowed to flow naturally. Her primary focus is public art and encouraging the education and growth of artistic creativity and empowerment in the local communities.
